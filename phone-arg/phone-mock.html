Shuttai 4h
People would find things to discriminate about even without quirks. Think about skin color, ethnicity, sex, height, IQ. I don't mean to speak directly against Prelude's personal ideals, but IMO removing people's quirks will in no way make the world a better place. All you're doing is trying to take away something that most people identify, consciously or unconsciously, as an integral part of themselves.

I've had the opportunity to personally interview people who have unfortunately lost their quirk due to various circumstances. The majority of them are listless and have difficulty concentrating. Difficulty holding down a job. They often develop depression and some fall into suicidal idealization. This INCLUDES people who were documented as saying quirk didn't really matter to them, and that even before they lost it they didn't use it much. They claimed they wouldn't, and didn't, miss their quirk.

I'm also not saying people without quirks are useless; I'm saying that quirks are embedded in DNA, and removing the quirk from a person can have INCREDIBLY unknown and unintended side effects, and that this is a much more complicated issue than people have recently been making it out to be.
